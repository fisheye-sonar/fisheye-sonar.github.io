Project Context

The use of sonar cameras to monitor salmon returning to spawn in watersheds from Alaska to California is a favored methodology due to its accuracy, ability to work at night, in highly turbid conditions, and unlike other techniques, does not result in any fish mortality. However, the adoption of sonar is currently constrained due its annual operating cost - the video must be viewed, and the fish counted, manually.

We are working to develop an affordable and automated as well as a robust and accurate system which will automate this process. By improving salmon monitoring, the fishery agencies responsible for the recovery of threatened salmon populations will be aided in their efforts, and the commercial management of healthy salmon populations will be better able to assure future catch rates that are sustainable.

Source: Trout Unlimited
